About our Sanctuary

Our Give a Hoot sanctuary nicknamed "The Parliament" has been a home to displaced owls since 2013. We noticed a need when more and more owls were losing their homes due to construction and land development across the country. While our main goal is to always release the owls we rescue back into the wild, that's not always possible due to circumstances. We decided something needed to be done for the owls that are well enough to survive yet not ready or able to live in the wild.

Once it was decided that we would house the owls here with us, our team came together and months of planning went into making sure every detail was worked out. We wanted to make their new home as close as possible to their natural habitat as possible so we brought in the experts on everything from the structure itself to the special environment needed and everything in between. Every aspect of the sanctuary was built with the variety of owls and their individual needs and habits in mind. Whether its an owl that lives high in the treetops or an owl that burrows in the ground the Give a Hoot sanctuary has enough love and a home for any of them.
