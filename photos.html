Did you Know?

  • Owls ears are asymmetrical.

  • There are more than 200 species of owls. Which are placed in two categories, barn owls or other.

  • Owls cannot move their eyes. Their eyes are "tubes" which provide great depth perception for hunting.

  • Owls have three eyelids, one for blinking, one for sleeping, and one for keeping their eyes clean and healthy.

  • Barn Owls eat up to 1,000 mice a year. Farmers use tricks to attract Barn Owls to help control rodents in their fields.

  • Owls are carnivorous and will eat rodents, insects, fish and other birds, including other owls.

  • Owls have zygodactyl feet with two toes pointing forward and two toes pointing backward.

  • Femals are larger and more agressive than the males.

  • Not all owls hoot. They make a wide range of noises such as screeches, whistles, barks and even growls.

  • Not all owls are nocturnal. It depends on the seasonal amount of daylight.

  • Most owls do not migrate.

  • A group of owls is called a parliament, bazaar, wisdom or study.

  • Baby owls are called owlets.
